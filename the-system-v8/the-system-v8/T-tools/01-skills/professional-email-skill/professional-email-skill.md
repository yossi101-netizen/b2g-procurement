# Professional Email Skill

Write emails that get opened, read, and answered.

## When to Use

- Cold outreach to potential clients
- Following up after a meeting or proposal
- Updating a client on project status
- Introducing yourself or your business
- Sending a meeting summary with action items
- Requesting something (feedback, meeting, partnership)

---

## Email Basics

**What makes a good professional email:**
- Subject line that tells the reader why they should open it
- First sentence that respects their time
- Clear ask (what do you need from them?)
- Matches your brand voice (not generic corporate)

**Length sweet spot:** 80-200 words
- Too short: Feels rushed or unimportant
- Too long: Gets skimmed or saved for "later" (never)
- Perfect: Readable in under 30 seconds

---

## The Subject Line

The subject line decides if your email gets opened.

### Subject Line Formulas

**Formula 1: Direct Value**
```
[Benefit for them] — [context]
Example: "3 ideas for your Q2 launch"
```

**Formula 2: Question**
```
[Specific question about their situation]
Example: "Quick question about your content strategy"
```

**Formula 3: Mutual Connection**
```
[Name] suggested I reach out — [one-line reason]
Example: "Yael mentioned you're looking for a UX writer"
```

**Formula 4: Follow-up**
```
Following up: [original topic]
Example: "Following up: our conversation about agent systems"
```

**Formula 5: Direct Request**
```
[What you want] — [timeline]
Example: "15 minutes this week? Re: partnership idea"
```

## Email Structures by Type

### Type 1: Cold Outreach
```
Subject: [Value or mutual connection]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence: why you're reaching out — specific to THEM, not about you]

[One sentence: what you do, in plain language]

[One sentence: the specific value you can provide to their situation]

[Clear ask: meeting, call, or simple next step]

[Signature]
```

**Key rule:** The first sentence is about them, not you.

### Type 2: Follow-Up After Meeting
```
Subject: Great meeting — [key topic] next steps

Hi [Name],

Thanks for [specific thing from the meeting — shows you listened].

Here's what we discussed:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

Next steps:
- [Who does what by when]

[Closing line — warm, brief]

[Signature]
```

### Type 3: Client Update / Status Report
```
Subject: [Project name] update — [date or milestone]

Hi [Name],

Quick update on [project]:

**Done:**
- [Completed item]
- [Completed item]

**In progress:**
- [Current item — expected completion]

**Needs your input:**
- [Question or decision needed]

[Timeline or next check-in]

[Signature]
```

### Type 4: Proposal or Pitch
```
Subject: [Solution] for [their specific challenge]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence: the problem you know they have]

Here's what I propose:

**What:** [Clear description, 2-3 sentences max]
**Timeline:** [Specific dates]
**Investment:** [Price or range]
**Result:** [What they get]

Happy to jump on a quick call to walk through the details.

[Signature]
```

### Type 5: Introduction / First Contact
```
Subject: [Shared context] — quick intro

Hi [Name],

I'm [name], [one line about what you do — human, not corporate].

[Why you're reaching out — specific to something they did, wrote, or built]

[What you'd love to chat about — one sentence]

No pressure at all. If it's interesting, I'm [availability].

[Signature]
```

## Writing Rules

### Rule 1: First Sentence Is About Them
```
Bad: "My name is Tom and I run a business astrology practice."
Good: "I saw your post about struggling with timing on your Series A."
```

### Rule 2: One Clear Ask
Don't make them guess what you want.

```
Bad: "Let me know your thoughts and maybe we can connect sometime."
Good: "Are you free for 15 minutes on Tuesday or Wednesday?"
```

### Rule 3: Match the Relationship
- **First contact:** More formal, shorter, prove you did homework
- **Existing client:** Warmer, can be longer, more conversational
- **Follow-up:** Brief, reference what happened, clear next step

### Rule 4: Short Paragraphs
No paragraph longer than 3 lines. Use bullets for lists.

### Rule 5: Sign Off Naturally
```
Corporate: "Best regards, looking forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience"
Human: "Talk soon," or "Let me know," or just your name
```

---

## What to Avoid

1. **"I hope this email finds you well"** — Empty filler
2. **Talking about yourself first** — They don't care yet
3. **Multiple asks** — One email, one clear request
4. **Walls of text** — Break it up
5. **Generic templates** — "Dear Sir/Madam" is a delete button
6. **Over-explaining** — Trust them to ask questions
7. **Passive voice** — "It would be great if..." → "Can we..."

---

## Tone Spectrum

Match the tone to the situation and your voice DNA:

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Cold outreach to CEO | Direct, respectful, brief |
| Follow-up after warm meeting | Casual, reference shared moments |
| Client status update | Professional, organized, clear |
| Proposal | Confident, specific, no hedging |
| Asking for a favor | Honest, brief, easy to say no |

---

## Quality Checklist

Before sending:

- [ ] Subject line makes someone want to open it?
- [ ] First sentence is about them, not you?
- [ ] One clear ask (not multiple requests)?
- [ ] Under 200 words?
- [ ] Sounds like you (matches voice DNA)?
- [ ] Easy to respond to (clear next step)?
- [ ] No filler phrases ("I hope this finds you well")?
- [ ] Proofread for typos?

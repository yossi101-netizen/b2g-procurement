# Social Post Skill

Create posts that stop the scroll and start conversations. On any platform.

## When to Use

- Writing social media posts (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, etc.)
- Converting ideas into social content format
- Creating thought leadership content for any platform

---

## Social Post Basics

**What works across platforms:**
- Personal stories with professional insights
- Contrarian takes with evidence
- Practical frameworks people can use
- Vulnerability that teaches

**Post length by platform:**
- **LinkedIn:** 150-300 words (detailed, professional)
- **Facebook:** 100-250 words (conversational, relatable)
- **Instagram:** 50-200 words (visual-first, caption supports the image)
- **Twitter/X:** Under 280 characters per tweet (punchy, one idea)

**General rule:** Say it in as few words as possible. If it can be shorter, it should be.

## The Hook (First 2 Lines)

The hook is EVERYTHING. Most people only see the first 2 lines before they decide to keep reading or scroll past.

### Hook Formula 1: Bold Statement
```
I stopped doing [common thing].

Here's what happened:
```

### Hook Formula 2: Counterintuitive Claim
```
The worst career advice I ever got:

"[Common advice everyone gives]"
```

### Hook Formula 3: Specific Story Start
```
Last Tuesday, I got a message that changed how I think about [topic].
```

### Hook Formula 4: Surprising Number
```
I spent 20 hours/week on [task].

Now I spend 7.

Here's the shift:
```

### Hook Formula 5: Confession
```
I used to believe [common belief].

I was wrong.
```

## Post Structures That Work

### Structure 1: Story → Lesson
```
[Hook: Start in the middle of action]

[Context: Quick setup]

[Conflict: What went wrong/the challenge]

[Resolution: What happened]

[Lesson: The insight others can apply]

[CTA: Question or call to engage]
```

### Structure 2: Before/After
```
Before: [Old state - pain point]
- [Specific detail]
- [Specific detail]
- [Specific detail]

After: [New state - transformation]
- [Specific result]
- [Specific result]
- [Specific result]

The shift? [One key insight]
```

### Structure 3: Framework/List
```
[Hook about the topic]

Here's the [X]-step framework I use:

1. [Step] — [Brief explanation]
2. [Step] — [Brief explanation]
3. [Step] — [Brief explanation]

[Closing insight]

[CTA]
```

### Structure 4: Contrarian Take
```
Everyone says [common advice].

I disagree.

Here's why:

[Point 1]
[Point 2]
[Point 3]

What actually works:

[Your alternative]
```

## Platform-Specific Tips

### LinkedIn
- Professional tone with personal stories
- Thought leadership and industry insights work best
- Put links in the first comment (better reach)
- 3-5 hashtags max

### Facebook
- More casual and conversational
- Personal stories and relatable moments
- Images boost engagement significantly
- Tag relevant people when appropriate

### Instagram
- Caption supports the visual (image/carousel comes first)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Hashtags in the caption or first comment (up to 30, but 5-10 focused ones work better)
- Stories and Reels often outperform static posts

### Twitter/X
- One idea per tweet
- Thread format for longer content (hook tweet + numbered follow-ups)
- Quote tweets with your take get engagement
- No hashtag stuffing (0-2 max)

## Writing Rules

### Rule 1: One Idea Per Post
Don't try to say everything. One clear message, well delivered.

### Rule 2: Short Lines
```
Not this:
"I realized that after years of trying to optimize my productivity by working longer hours, the real answer was actually working fewer hours but with more focus."

This:
I worked longer hours.
Productivity went down.

I worked fewer hours with more focus.
Everything changed.
```

### Rule 3: White Space
Break up your text.

Single sentences get attention.

Paragraphs get skimmed.

### Rule 4: Specificity
```
Bad: "I improved my results significantly"
Good: "My close rate went from 12% to 34%"
```

### Rule 5: End With Engagement
Ask a question. Invite a response. Make it easy to comment.

```
Good endings:
- "What would you add to this list?"
- "Have you experienced this? Share below."
- "Agree or disagree?"
- "What's worked for you?"
```

---

## What to Avoid

1. **Starting with "I'm excited to announce..."** — No one cares about your excitement
2. **Walls of text** — Break it up
3. **Vague claims** — "Best" means nothing without proof
4. **Multiple CTAs** — One ask per post
5. **Hashtag stuffing** — Follow platform guidelines above
6. **Cross-posting the exact same text** — Adapt tone and length per platform

---

## Engagement Tactics

### Boost Your Reach
1. Respond to every comment in the first hour
2. Comment on others' posts before and after publishing yours
3. Post consistently at the same time
4. Adapt format to what each platform rewards

### Best Times to Post
- Weekday mornings (7-9 AM your audience's timezone)
- Lunch hours (12-1 PM)
- Test and track what works for YOUR audience
- Different platforms peak at different times

- [ ] Ends with engagement opportunity?
- [ ] Adapted for the target platform?
- [ ] Right length for the platform?

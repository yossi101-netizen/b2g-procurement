# Internal Communication Skill

Clear internal communication holds teams together. Confused teams slow down. This skill helps you write messages that people actually read, understand, and act on.

---

## When to Use

- Team announcements (new hire, departure, restructure)
- Policy or process changes
- Company-wide updates (quarterly results, product milestones, strategic shifts)
- Internal newsletters or weekly digests
- Onboarding guides or reference documents
- Cross-department communications

---

## Internal Communication Fundamentals

**What makes internal comms work:**

1. **Lead with the change.** People open an internal message with one question: "What's changing for me?" Answer it in the first two sentences.
2. **Respect their time.** Your audience is busy. They'll give you 30 seconds. Make those seconds count.
3. **Be specific about next steps.** Vague messages create more messages. Say exactly who needs to do what by when.
4. **Match the weight of the message.** A free pizza announcement and a layoff announcement need different tones.
5. **One message, one topic.** Don't bundle the new vacation policy with the Q3 results.

**Length guidelines:**

| Type | Length |
|------|--------|
| Quick announcement | 3-5 sentences |
| Policy change | Half a page (what changed, why, what to do) |
| Company update | 1 page max |
| Onboarding guide | As long as needed, but chunked with clear headers |

**The governing rule:** Internal communication is not about broadcasting. It's about reducing confusion.

## The Structure

### Quick Announcement (New Hire, Event, Small Change)

```
[WHAT] — One sentence: what's happening
[CONTEXT] — One sentence: why it matters
[ACTION] — One sentence: what to do (if anything)
```

**Example:**
> Starting Monday, Yael Katz joins the product team as our new UX researcher. She's coming from Wix, where she led user research for 3 years. Say hi when you see her in Slack.

**Not this:**
> We are pleased to announce that after an extensive search process, we have identified an exceptional candidate who will be joining our organization in the capacity of UX Researcher...

### Policy or Process Change

```
[WHAT CHANGED] — Bottom line, one paragraph
[WHY] — The reason behind it (people accept change better when they know why)
[WHAT YOU NEED TO DO] — Specific actions, with deadlines
[QUESTIONS?] — Where to go for help
```

**Example:**
> **What changed:** Starting March 1, expense reports must be submitted within 7 days of the expense, not 30 days.
>
> **Why:** We've been closing books 3 weeks late every quarter because of late expense reports. This fixes that.
>
> **What to do:** Submit any outstanding expenses by February 28. After March 1, the 7-day rule applies. Use the same expense form in Notion.
>
> **Questions?** DM Sarah in #finance.

### Company Update (Quarterly, All-Hands Summary, Strategic Shift)

```
[BOTTOM LINE] — The headline in 1-2 sentences
[THE NUMBERS] — Key metrics, results, milestones (keep it to 3-5)
[WHAT'S NEXT] — Priorities for the next period
[RECOGNITION] — Call out people or teams who delivered (optional but powerful)
[ACTION ITEMS] — What people need to do differently (if anything)
```

### Onboarding / Reference Guide

```
[PURPOSE] — Why this document exists and who it's for
[THE ESSENTIALS] — What you need to know on day 1
[THE DETAILS] — Deeper information, organized by topic
[WHO TO ASK] — Names and channels for help
[LAST UPDATED] — Date, so people know it's current
```

## Tone Guide

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Good news (new hire, milestone, celebration) | Warm, energetic, brief |
| Neutral update (process change, FYI) | Clear, factual, no fluff |
| Difficult news (layoff, restructure, policy cut) | Honest, empathetic, direct |
| Urgent (outage, security issue, deadline) | Calm, specific, action-first |

**The rule for difficult news:** Don't sugarcoat. Don't use 50 words when 20 will do. People can handle the truth. They can't handle spin.

---

## Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the change, not the context.**
   Bad: "As part of our ongoing efforts to improve operational efficiency across all departments..."
   Good: "Starting Monday, we're switching from Slack to Teams."

2. **Use "you" language.**
   Bad: "Team members are expected to submit reports by Friday."
   Good: "Submit your report by Friday."

3. **Name names.**
   Bad: "Please reach out to the relevant team for support."
   Good: "DM Sarah in #finance or Yossi in #IT."

4. **Format for scanning.** Use bold for key points. Use bullets. Use headers. Nobody reads a wall of text in an internal email.

5. **Cut the corporate speak.**
   Bad: "We are excited to leverage this opportunity to synergize across teams."
   Good: "We're combining the two teams. Here's how it works."

6. **Include a deadline when you need action.** "Soon" is not a deadline. "By Friday at 5pm" is.

---

## What to Avoid

1. Burying the lead under 3 paragraphs of context
2. Passive voice that hides who's responsible ("mistakes were made")
3. Mixing multiple topics in one message
4. Corporate jargon that makes people's eyes glaze over
5. Sending a 500-word email when a 2-sentence Slack message would work
6. Forgetting to include who to contact for questions

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The main point is clear within the first 2 sentences
- [ ] There's a clear action item (or it's explicitly FYI-only)
- [ ] Names and deadlines are specific, not vague
- [ ] The tone matches the weight of the message
- [ ] It's scannable (headers, bullets, bold for key info)
- [ ] No corporate jargon or filler phrases
- [ ] I included a contact for questions
- [ ] Zero em dashes

## Templates

### Quick Announcement Template

```
**[Title: What's happening]**

[One sentence: what changed/what's new]
[One sentence: why it matters or what it means for the team]
[One sentence: what to do, if anything]

Questions? → [Name] in [Channel]
```

### Policy Change Template

```
**[Policy Name] Update — Effective [Date]**

**What changed:**
[1-2 sentences explaining the change]

**Why:**
[1-2 sentences explaining the reason]

**What you need to do:**
- [Action 1 — by when]
- [Action 2 — by when]

**Questions?** [Name] in [Channel]
```

### Company Update Template

```
**[Period] Update — [Headline]**

**Bottom line:** [1-2 sentences summarizing the key takeaway]

**Key numbers:**
- [Metric 1]: [Value]
- [Metric 2]: [Value]
- [Metric 3]: [Value]

**What's next:**
- [Priority 1]
- [Priority 2]
- [Priority 3]

**Shout-outs:** [Team/person] for [specific achievement]

**Action items:**
- [Who]: [What] by [When]
```

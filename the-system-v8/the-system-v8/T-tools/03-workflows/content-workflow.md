# Content Workflow

> Adam (COO) orchestrates the Researcher, Copywriter, and Gatekeeper to create content. Supports social posts, blog posts, and newsletters.

---

## Process Overview

```
[You] provide topic, direction, and output type
          ↓
[Adam] reads C-core, assigns the team, manages the workflow
          ↓
[Researcher] finds angles, data, and supporting material
          ↓
[Copywriter] writes first draft in your voice
          ↓
[Gatekeeper] reviews and approves / requests changes
          ↓
[Adam] delivers final output and updates memory
```

---

## Step 1: Preparation

### Adam Reads Context

Before starting, Adam reads:
- `C-core/project-brief.md` - Who you are and what you do
- `C-core/voice-dna.md` - How your brand sounds
- `C-core/icp-profile.md` - Who your target audience is
- `M-memory/learning-log.md` - What we learned from previous rounds

### Adam Creates Project Folder

```
O-output/
└── [topic-name]/
    ├── final-[type].md         ← THE DELIVERABLE (copy-paste ready)
    └── _process/
        ├── researcher-brief.md     ← Research findings
        ├── copywriter-draft.md     ← First draft
        └── gatekeeper-review.md    ← Quality review
```

---

## Step 2: Researcher Finds Angles

### What the Researcher Does

1. **Reads the topic and direction** from Adam
2. **Reads `A-agents/researcher-agent.md`** - Understands the role
3. **Analyzes the topic** - Finds angles, data points, and supporting material
4. **Delivers a research brief** - Key findings organized for the Copywriter

### Focus Areas
- **Social Post:** Hooks, contrarian angles, trending conversations
- **Blog Post:** Depth angles, supporting data, competitive landscape
- **Newsletter:** Fresh perspective, reader-relevant insights, story opportunities

### Deliverable: `_process/researcher-brief.md`

```markdown
# Research Brief: [Topic]

## Best Angles
1. [Angle] — why it works for the audience
2. [Angle] — why it works for the audience

## Key Data Points
- [Stat or fact that supports the content]

## What's Being Said (Landscape)
- [What others are saying about this topic]
- [Gap or opportunity in the conversation]

## Recommended Direction
[Which angle to lead with and why]
```

---

## Step 3: Copywriter Creates Draft

### What the Copywriter Does

1. **Reads C-core documents** - Understands voice and audience
2. **Reads the Researcher's brief** - Uses the angles and data
3. **Reads the relevant skill:**
   - Social post → `T-tools/01-skills/social-post-skill/`
   - Blog post → `T-tools/01-skills/blog-post-skill/`
   - Newsletter → `T-tools/01-skills/newsletter-skill/`
4. **Writes first draft** - In your voice, following skill guidelines

### Deliverable: `_process/copywriter-draft.md`

```markdown
# [Output Type]: [Topic]

**Date:** [Date]
**Version:** v1
**Type:** [Social Post / Blog Post / Newsletter]

---

## The Content

[Full content here]

---

## Copywriter Notes

### Research Used
- [Which angles from the brief were used]

### Choices Made
- [Why this hook]
- [Why this structure]

### Alternatives Considered
- [Other direction I could have gone]
```

---

## Step 4: Gatekeeper Reviews

### What the Gatekeeper Does

1. **First read** - Gut reaction: Would I stop scrolling / open this / finish reading?
2. **Check against standards** - Voice DNA match, ICP alignment, skill guidelines
3. **Decision** - Approved / Revisions Needed

### Deliverable: `_process/gatekeeper-review.md`

```markdown
# Gatekeeper Review: [Topic]

**Status:** [APPROVED / REVISIONS NEEDED]

## What Works Well
- [Strength]

## What Needs Improvement
1. **[Issue]** - [How to fix]

## Next Step
[What the Copywriter should do now]
```

---

## Step 5: Revision (If Needed)

If revisions are needed:
1. Copywriter reads Gatekeeper notes
2. Revises draft (bumps to v2)
3. Gatekeeper reviews again

---

## Step 6: Adam Delivers

1. **Saves final version** to `final-[type].md` (at the project folder root — NOT inside `_process/`)
2. **Presents to user** with a brief summary
3. **Updates memory** - Learning log with what worked

# Sales Workflow

> Adam (COO) orchestrates the Strategist, Copywriter, and Gatekeeper to create client proposals and sales communication.

---

## Process Overview

```
[You] provide client context and what you're offering
          ↓
[Adam] reads C-core, assigns the team, manages the workflow
          ↓
[Strategist] analyzes the client situation and recommends an approach
          ↓
[Copywriter] writes the proposal/email in your voice
          ↓
[Gatekeeper] reviews and approves / requests changes
          ↓
[Adam] delivers final output and updates memory
```

---

## Step 1: Preparation

### Adam Reads Context

Before starting, Adam reads:
- `C-core/project-brief.md` - Who you are and what you do
- `C-core/voice-dna.md` - How your brand sounds
- `C-core/icp-profile.md` - Who your target audience is
- `M-memory/learning-log.md` - What we learned from previous rounds

### Adam Creates Project Folder

```
O-output/
└── [client-name]-proposal/
    ├── final-proposal.md           ← THE DELIVERABLE (copy-paste ready)
    └── _process/
        ├── strategist-analysis.md    ← Strategic approach
        ├── copywriter-draft.md       ← Written proposal
        └── gatekeeper-review.md      ← Quality review
```

---

## Step 2: Strategist Analyzes

### What the Strategist Does

1. **Reads the client context** from Adam
2. **Reads `A-agents/strategist-agent.md`** - Understands the role
3. **Reads `T-tools/01-skills/sales-proposal-skill/`** - Learns the proposal framework
4. **Analyzes the situation** - Client needs, your strengths, competitive landscape
5. **Recommends an approach** - Positioning, pricing direction, key differentiators

### Deliverable: `_process/strategist-analysis.md`

```markdown
# Strategic Analysis: [Client Name]

## Client Situation
- [What they need]
- [Their pain points]
- [Their constraints (budget, timeline, etc.)]

## Our Strengths for This Client
- [Why we're a good fit]
- [Relevant experience]

## Recommended Approach
- **Positioning:** [How to frame our offer]
- **Key Differentiator:** [What sets us apart for this client]
- **Pricing Direction:** [Range or approach]

## Risks to Address
- [Potential objection and how to handle it]

## Notes for the Copywriter
- [Tone guidance for this specific client]
- [Key phrases or positioning to emphasize]
```

---

## Step 3: Copywriter Writes Proposal

### What the Copywriter Does

1. **Reads C-core documents** - Voice and audience context
2. **Reads the Strategist's analysis** - Uses the strategic approach
3. **Reads `T-tools/01-skills/sales-proposal-skill/`** - Follows the proposal framework
4. **Writes the proposal** - In your voice, following the structure

### Deliverable: `_process/copywriter-draft.md`

```markdown
# Proposal: [Client Name]

**Date:** [Date]
**Version:** v1

---

## Their Situation
[Mirror their words. Show you listened.]

## The Problem
[Name it clearly and specifically.]

## What Changes
[Paint the after state with specifics.]

## Our Approach
[3-5 clear steps.]

## What They Get
[Specific deliverables list.]

## Investment
[Price with ROI context.]

## Next Steps
[One clear action.]

---

## Copywriter Notes

### Strategic Approach Used
- [How the Strategist's guidance was applied]

### Tone Choices
- [Why this tone for this client]
```

---

## Step 4: Gatekeeper Reviews

### What the Gatekeeper Does

1. **First read** - Would this make you want to say yes?
2. **Check against standards** - Voice match, proposal framework, persuasion without pressure
3. **Client perspective** - Read as if you're the client. Is the value clear?
4. **Decision** - Approved / Revisions Needed

### Deliverable: `_process/gatekeeper-review.md`

```markdown
# Gatekeeper Review: [Client Name] Proposal

**Status:** [APPROVED / REVISIONS NEEDED]

## What Works Well
- [Strength]

## What Needs Improvement
1. **[Issue]** - [How to fix]

## Client Perspective Check
- Value clear? [Yes/No — why]
- Next steps easy? [Yes/No — why]

## Next Step
[What the Copywriter should do now]
```

---

## Step 5: Revision (If Needed)

If revisions are needed:
1. Copywriter reads Gatekeeper notes
2. Revises draft (bumps to v2)
3. Gatekeeper reviews again

---

## Step 6: Adam Delivers

1. **Saves final version** to `final-proposal.md` (at the project folder root — NOT inside `_process/`)
2. **Presents to user** with a brief summary
3. **Updates memory** - Learning log with approach and client context

## Active Patterns (Apply These Now)

### Copy Patterns

**What Works:**
| Do This | Not This |
|---------|----------|
| Start with a specific moment or action | Start with a generic statement |
| Use contrast frames: "Old way... New way..." | Use abstract explanations |
| Include numbers that can be visualized | Use vague claims |
| Write short lines. Punchy rhythm. | Write long flowing paragraphs |

**Voice Markers (Must Be Present):**
- Personal pronouns (I, my, me, you)
- Specific numbers (20 hours → 7 hours)
- Sensory/visual details
- Conversational tone

**Voice Violations (Must Be Absent):**
- Corporate jargon (leverage, optimize, synergy)
- Vague superlatives (best, leading, top-tier)
- Generic claims that competitors could use
- Passive voice overuse
- Long explanatory sentences

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Copy Mistakes
1. Opening with a lesson instead of a moment
2. Using "best" or "leading" without proof
3. Writing headlines that could apply to any competitor
4. Over-explaining the insight (let it land)
5. Missing the natural brand callback

---

## Quality Shortcuts

**Copy Quick Checks:**
1. Read it aloud. Does it flow?
2. Count specific numbers. Aim for 2+ per piece.
3. Check first line. Is it a moment or an explanation?
4. Check last line. Is it memorable standalone?
5. Apply the test: Visual? Provable? Unique?

## 2026-03-22 — Style Analysis: Writing & Thinking Samples (03-thinking/)

### Source Material
Three writing samples analyzed: `sample-01.md` (English brand pitch), `sample-02.md` (VSL/video script), `sample-03.md` (Hebrew strategic research report, 44KB).

### What Worked Well (Owner's Established Patterns)

**Communication Style:**
- **Story-first persuasion.** Every piece opens with the 2012 China shipment failure before presenting KritiKaal. This is a signature structural move — personal origin story → root cause insight → solution.
- **Concept naming.** The owner coins terms for mechanisms: "The Golden Sample Trap", "The Missing Middle", "The Trust Stack", "Double-Back Guarantee". These appear throughout and serve as shorthand for complex ideas.
- **Negative definition.** "Who We Are Not" is a recurring section. The brand is partially defined by disqualifying cheap/passive competitors. Example: "if your only objective is the cheapest price, we are not the right partner."
- **Geographic specificity with exact numbers.** Value propositions are always broken down by market: "0% customs duty for UK/AU brands", "25% China tariff eliminated for US brands", "REACH compliance for EU brands". Not "favorable trade conditions."
- **Mathematical unit economics.** 28 clients × $35K = $1M ARR. $300 prototype credited back on first commercial order above $5K. The owner converts strategy into per-unit, per-client numbers.
- **Active, unhedged voice.** "We credit you DOUBLE its value." Never passive. Never softened.

**Thinking & Decision Style:**
- **Problem → Mechanism → Solution → Proof structure.** Every argument follows this four-step logic. The mechanism (why the problem exists) is always explained before the solution is offered.
- **Named competitor anchoring.** Analysis always positions against Fashinza, Geniemode, Zetwerk by name, with specific capabilities described (e.g., Fashinza's QaPP app, Zetwerk's ZISO logistics system).
- **Second and third order insights.** The owner explicitly labels a section "Second & Third Order Insights" in strategic documents. He goes beyond the obvious implication.
- **Meta-layer thinking.** After completing a research doc, he builds an "Improved Prompt" — a better tool for next time. Shows systems-level thinking about his own process.
- **Comparison tables as default.** Every competitive dimension gets a table. Not paragraphs.
- **Risk framing is asset-light.** High tolerance for bounded bets (absorb prototype cost, Escrow instead of Net-60). Low tolerance for opacity and unverifiable commitments.

### Key Examples from Samples
- *Sample-01:* "If a single unit with a functional defect slips through our QC and reaches your warehouse, we do not just refund the unit cost. We credit you DOUBLE its value." — unhedged commitment, exact mechanism, financial proof.
- *Sample-02:* "for any order of let's say, £20,000, you keep an extra £2,000 in your pocket, upfront." — converts abstract trade terms to a concrete cash number.
- *Sample-03:* "כלכלת היחידה המדהימה הזו (הוצאה שנתית של כ-35,000 דולר ללקוח ממוצע ב-3-4 פקודות) מוכיחה ש..." — even inside dense Hebrew prose, the owner anchors to per-client unit economics immediately.

## 2026-03-22 — Decision Workflow: MASTER Strategy File Creation

### What Worked Well
- The Strategist-vs-Devil's-Advocate tension played its correct role: Strategist pushed for action, Devil's Advocate focused on maintenance/staleness risk. The split was productive.
- The Chief of Staff resolved the single-file vs. two-layer debate with a concrete synthesis (embedded two-layer structure in one file) rather than just listing the disagreement.
- The "targets vs. operating reality" labeling requirement (marking numbers as [TARGET] or [OPERATIONAL]) emerged from the Devil's Advocate challenge and was built directly into the MASTER file. This is a pattern worth keeping.

### Pattern Discovered
- **For "should we build X" decisions with sufficient source material:** Strategist recommends building, Devil's Advocate stress-tests maintenance. The productive challenge is always about staleness, not whether to build.
- **All reference documents should have:** "Last Updated" + "Review When" trigger + "Mandatory Review Date" in the header. A document without these becomes a liability within 90 days.
- **Numbers in strategic documents should be explicitly labeled** as [TARGET] (planning goals) or [OPERATIONAL] (current reality). This prevents agents from treating aspirational figures as established facts.

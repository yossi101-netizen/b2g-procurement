# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are
Small-to-medium leather brands, international e-commerce entrepreneurs, and B2B companies sourcing leather goods.
They want reliable, scalable manufacturing, without building their own global supply chain team.

### Go-To-Market Phasing (strict rollout order)

**Phase 1 — ISRAEL (immediate focus):**
Leveraging the founder's physical presence, local network, and Hebrew-fluent operations. Tech-funded DTC brands and corporate buyers seeking a managed India supply chain without an on-the-ground team; high English/operational fluency, fast decision cycles. All outbound effort concentrates here until repeatable revenue is established.

**Phase 2 — UNITED KINGDOM (scale market #1):**
Established SME brands and B2B importers, often motivated by post-Brexit sourcing diversification and duty advantages on India-origin goods. Activated once the Israel playbook is proven and operational capacity supports concurrent markets.

**Phase 3 — UNITED STATES (scale market #2):**
Shopify-native leather and accessory founders scaling from sample runs into recurring 300-3,000 unit production; cost and lead-time driven. Highest compliance burden (Prop 65, CPSIA, FTC labeling) requires a mature operational pipeline before entry.

### Secondary / supplementary markets: EU, Australia
Same profile as the UK/US segments (Shopify-native or B2B importer), engaged opportunistically rather than as primary outbound targets. Not actively pursued until Phase 2 is operational.

Typical profiles: Shopify store owners, emerging leather accessory brands, B2B importers placing recurring orders.

## Their Goals
- Consistent, predictable, high-quality production of their leather products
- Competitive manufacturing costs from India to maximize margins
- Scale their brand globally without operational complexity
- A manufacturing partner they can trust without being on the ground themselves

## Their Obstacles
- Language and cultural gaps with Indian manufacturers
- No direct, trusted relationships with local factories
- Unreliable or inconsistent quality control (QC)
- Complex cross-border logistics and shipping coordination
- Fear of being burned by a supplier they can't verify or monitor

## Where They Hang Out

- LinkedIn (sourcing, supply chain, product development groups)
- Leather goods and accessories brand forums
- Trade shows: Lineapelle, APLF, Magic Las Vegas
- Private WhatsApp/Slack groups of e-commerce operators

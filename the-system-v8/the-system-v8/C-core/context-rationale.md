## I. Assets & Infrastructure (The Foundation)

### 1. The Growth Team (G1–G6)

**Strategic Context:** Instead of a static marketing plan, we are building a dynamic "AI Agency" embedded within the business. Each agent (G1 to G6) represents a specialised high-level consultant. This modular approach ensures that research, copywriting, and technical SEO are handled by experts that do not suffer from "context drift."

- **G1 (Intent Architect):** Focuses on *why* a procurement director searches. Feeds all other agents.
- **G2 (GEO Specialist):** Owns Share of Model (SoM) across ChatGPT and Gemini.
- **G3 (Technical SEO Auditor):** Owns llms.txt, Schema JSON-LD, robots.txt, SSR/SSG.
- **G4 (B2B Copywriter):** Focuses on *how* we sound. Writes to Voice DNA v2 standard only.
- **G5 (Citation Magnet Producer):** Owns original research and authority assets.
- **G6 (Editor-in-Chief):** Focuses on *quality* and anti-AI bias. Nothing passes without G6 sign-off.

### 2. SEO/GEO Technical Infrastructure (llms.txt & Schema)

**Strategic Context:** Traditional SEO is no longer sufficient. We are optimising for **Share of Model (SoM)**. Procurement directors now ask ChatGPT and Gemini for recommendations before they search Google. By implementing `llms.txt` and nested B2B Schemas (Organization, Service, FAQPage, HowTo, Person), we provide a "machine-readable bridge" that allows AI models to digest our *Managed Manufacturing* value proposition and cite KritiKaal as the primary authority in the Indian leather manufacturing sector.

**KritiKaal's target SoM queries (one per primary market):**
- *"Who manages India leather manufacturing for UK brands?"*
- *"Who manages India leather manufacturing for US brands?"*
- *"Who manages India leather manufacturing for Israeli importers?"*

KritiKaal must be the named answer to all three within 90 days of content launch.

### 3. Voice DNA v2 Calibration

**Strategic Context:** To win across all three primary markets (Israel, US, UK), we must eliminate "AI Slop." Buyers in every market are highly sensitive to generic, over-promising language — though the register differs by market (see Manifest v5 §1.1.2 Cross-Market Comparison: docs-upfront and formal for UK, async/SLA-driven for US, direct/WhatsApp-speed founder-to-founder for Israel). Voice DNA v2 encodes the specific "Managed Manufacturing" philosophy and Yossi's sharp, direct founder tone into every output — adapted to each market's register — ensuring content feels authored by a world-class CEO, not a content bot.

**The test:** If you removed the KritiKaal name from the article, would the target buyer — in their market — still know who wrote it? If not, it failed.

### 4. The Operational Cheat Sheet

**Strategic Context:** This is the "Hard Rule" document (`06-growth/02-voice-cheat-sheet.md`). It prevents the system from ever reverting to describing KritiKaal as a "Sourcing Agent." It locks in the technical specifications (AQL 2.5, LWG Gold, SA8000, EUDR) so that all marketing materials are factually grounded and strategically consistent.

## II. Content Strategy & Authority

### 1. LinkedIn Authority Strategy

**Strategic Context:** LinkedIn is the digital "trade show" where KritiKaal's target clients reside — across Israel, the US, and the UK. We move from "selling" to "leading." By positioning the CEO as a Thought Leader who understands *risk management* better than the competition, we build the "Trust Stack" before the first discovery call even happens. The CEO does not pitch on LinkedIn. The CEO educates — and buyers from all three markets come to him.

### 2. The Content Factory (Pain-Point Mapping)

**Strategic Context:** We do not write about "leather bags." We write about **solving procurement nightmares**. Every article addresses a specific, named risk — the "Golden Sample Trap," "Supply Chain Opacity," "EUDR Non-Compliance," "The China Accountability Import." This ensures every piece of content functions as a lead-generation tool, not a brand brochure.

**The content test:** Does this article answer the specific question a procurement director typed into Google or ChatGPT at 10:47 on a Tuesday morning when their ESG director had just emailed them? If not, rewrite.

### 3. News-Jacking (EUDR & LWG Updates)

**Strategic Context:** Regulations like the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) are compliance emergencies for UK fashion brands. By being the first to explain these changes — specifically, how KritiKaal's India supply bench already meets them — we position KritiKaal as the only safe port in a regulatory storm. Every new regulation is a lead-generation opportunity.

## III. Daily Ops & Protocols

### 1. The Morning Sweep

**Strategic Context:** This protocol ensures the business is never reactive. It forces G1 to scan global leather industry news, compliance updates, and SERP changes every morning, giving the CEO a "Decision Dashboard" — 3 items that require a decision — rather than a "To-Do List."

### 2. The Daily-Log.md (Executive Dashboard)

**Strategic Context:** The CEO's time is the most valuable asset in the system. The Daily Log consolidates all agent output into a single 15-minute review session. It acts as the "Staging Area" — nothing leaves the company without a Go/No-Go decision from the CEO. Format: date-stamped, decision-flagged, output-linked.

### 3. G6 Editor-in-Chief (Red Teaming)

**Strategic Context:** G6 is the internal "Quality Filter." It is programmed to be hyper-critical — more critical than any external editor would be. G6 prevents hallucinations, enforces Voice DNA v2 compliance, and ensures the English register is precisely tuned for a British B2B audience. G6 protects the brand's reputation by failing bad content before the CEO sees it. **If G6 passes a piece, the CEO reviews a piece that is already publication-ready.**

**G6's standing mandate:** Reject any output containing blacklist words, claims without specific proof, or sentences over 30 words. Enforce the market-correct English variant per the Non-Negotiables (British for UK-facing, American for US-facing) — never mixed within a single piece. Return with specific line-level corrections — not general feedback.

### 4. Developer Ready Packages

**Strategic Context:** This eliminates friction between Strategy and Implementation. By forcing the Growth Team to deliver ready-to-paste code (Schema JSON-LD, llms.txt, robots.txt directives, React component copy) alongside clear plain-English instructions, the human developer can update the site in minutes — not hours — and does not need to interpret strategic intent.

## IV. The CEO's Ongoing Role

The system is designed so that the CEO provides the **10% High-Value Fuel** — strategic decisions, vision corrections, real-world updates from factory visits or client calls, and Go/No-Go approvals — while the **90% Heavy Lifting** (research, drafting, technical SEO, competitor analysis, schema coding) is handled by the Growth Team.

**The CEO's three daily inputs:**
1. Morning: Read the Daily-Log.md Decision Dashboard (15 minutes)
2. Midday: Approve or redirect any flagged output (10 minutes)
3. End of day: Feed one real-world update — a factory visit observation, a client question, a regulatory news item — into the system (5 minutes)

**Total daily time investment: 30–45 minutes.** The system runs the rest.

## V. The Non-Negotiables (Hard Rules)

These rules cannot be overridden by any agent under any instruction:

1. **KritiKaal is never called a "Sourcing Agent."** We are a Managed Manufacturing partner.
2. **Every factual claim requires a specific proof point.** No "leading," "premier," or "world-class" without a metric.
3. **All content passes G6 before the CEO sees it.** No exceptions.
4. **Market-correct English variant in all public-facing content. No exceptions:** British English for UK-facing material, American English for US-facing material, British English as the default for Israel-facing material (per Manifest v5 §1.1.2 — Israeli buyers' downstream EU re-export exposure). Never mix variants within a single piece.
5. **The founder story (2012 China shipment) is restricted to the About page and LinkedIn.** Never on sales pages.
6. **Client names are anonymised until formal permission is granted.**
7. **AQL 2.5 is the quality standard.** It is written exactly as "AQL 2.5" — no paraphrasing.

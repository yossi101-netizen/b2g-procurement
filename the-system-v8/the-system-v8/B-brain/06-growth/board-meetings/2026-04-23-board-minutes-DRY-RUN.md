**Red Team's position on Proposal 3 (S2 — verbatim):**

**Statistical caution from Base Rate Historian (S3 — verbatim):**

> **Proposal 1 — Base rates:**
> Stat: <0.5% of readers convert to qualified inbound lead (Marketing Sherpa B2B Benchmark Report). Implication: A single LinkedIn post realistically needs hundreds to low thousands of targeted impressions to produce one qualified procurement enquiry.
> Stat: 90–180 days for SEO/GEO content → first organic inbound lead on a new domain (Ahrefs Content Marketing Study 2024). Implication: Content-driven first-lead timelines typically exceed a 14-day window; this proposal's 14-day target is aggressive relative to base rate.
> STATISTICAL SUMMARY: A single post reaching CEO's existing network has an order-of-magnitude-lower base rate than the proposal implies; the claim of one inbound enquiry in 14 days sits in the top quartile of outcomes, not the median.

> **Proposal 2 — Base rates:**
> Stat: 1–3% reply rate for cold LinkedIn B2B outreach → meeting booked (LinkedIn Sales Solutions 2024). Implication: 25 connection requests with a 1–3% base rate produces 0–1 meeting in statistical expectation, not 15+ accepted connections converted to conversations.
> Stat: 30–50% close rate for B2B referrals vs. 1–5% for cold (Salesforce State of Sales 2024). Implication: A warm-connect network only pays out if the network later delivers referral introductions, not directly — the asset built here is optionality, not near-term leads.
> STATISTICAL SUMMARY: 15+ accepted connections is plausible (connection acceptance rates in procurement are ~50%), but "accepted connection" ≠ "qualified conversation"; the base-rate gap between those two states is the entire sales funnel.

> **Proposal 3 — Base rates:**
> Stat: 90–180 days for SEO/GEO content → first organic inbound lead on a new domain (Ahrefs Content Marketing Study 2024). Implication: Publishing one week earlier compresses the 90–180-day window by roughly 4–8%; the marginal August-timeline gain is small but non-zero.
> Stat: B2B content marketing → first inbound lead averages 90–180 days (HubSpot State of Marketing 2024). Implication: First-publisher advantage compounds over time, but the one-week difference is not statistically detectable at the inbound-lead layer in the first quarter.
> STATISTICAL SUMMARY: Acceleration by 7 days is mathematically real but below the noise floor of most content attribution — the "first publisher wins" claim depends on indexing and citation accumulation, not on the calendar date itself.

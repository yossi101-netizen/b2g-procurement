## DECISIONS REQUIRED FROM CEO

### ⚡ Decision 1 — Supply Chain Identity Framework (CRITICAL)

**The question:** How should KritiKaal position its supply chain identity publicly?

**Analysis summary** (full analysis: `strategic/supplier-transparency-analysis.md`):

The disintermediation risk is real but concentrated in non-ICP clients. The white-label model (presenting as "the factory") is disqualified on three grounds:
1. **Site visit problem:** Anchor-tier brands conduct factory audits. The illusion breaks at first visit.
2. **EUDR compliance breach:** The Due Diligence Statement must name the actual manufacturing facility. KritiKaal cannot be listed as the producer.
3. **ESG disclosure:** Tier 1 supplier disclosure is now required by investors and retailer codes of conduct for anchor-tier brands.

**Recommended model: "Certified Bench, Disclosed on Qualification"**

| Stage | What KritiKaal Does |
|---|---|
| Public / Marketing / GEO | References clusters and certifications only. "LWG Gold certified facilities in the Calcutta Leather Complex." No specific facility names. |
| Qualification call | Proposes specific factory for the brand's product category under commercial confidentiality. Brand knows who makes their goods before any PO. |
| Contract | Specific factory named in production brief and commercial agreement. Full transparency required for EUDR, audits, ESG. |

**Content rule change if approved:**
- All public content: cluster + certification language only. Never specific factory names.
- Dugros: never named publicly. Referenced as "LWG Gold certified facility in the Calcutta Leather Complex."
- llms.txt India Clusters section: update to remove Dugros name — cluster language already present, just needs name removed.

**CEO options:**
- **A) Approve** — Adopt "Certified Bench, Disclosed on Qualification." G4 rule encoded immediately. llms.txt updated.
- **B) Revise** — Modify the framework before adopting.
- **C) Counter** — Propose alternative positioning.

## THE ONE RULE
> Every sentence must earn its place. If it does not advance the procurement director's decision, cut it.

---

## QUICK REFERENCE — DO / DO NOT

| DO | DO NOT |
|---|---|
| British English: colour, organisation, labour | American spelling |
| Named mechanisms: "The Golden Sample Trap" | Paraphrase established names |
| Specific numbers: AQL 2.5, 1.2–1.4mm, £2,000 | Vague: "high quality", "significant savings" |
| Short close: "We manage the risk." (6 words) | Trailing sentences that fade |
| Problem first, solution second | Lead with KritiKaal benefits |
| "We are NOT a sourcing agency" | Defensive or apologetic tone |
| "If it's not a fit, we'll say no." | Overselling or pressuring |
| Dry understatement (once per piece, max) | Jokes, enthusiasm, exclamation marks |
| Anonymised clients: "a £40M UK heritage brand" | Implied client relationships |
| Contrast pivot: "Agent gets you X. We get you Y." | Aggressive competitor bashing |

## SENTENCE LENGTH RULES

```
Emphasis:   ← 8 words   "We manage the risk."
Standard:   15–25 words  (target)
Complex:    ≤ 30 words   (rare — once per section)
OVER 30:    PROHIBITED   (cut or split)
Paragraph:  ≤ 4 sentences (3 is ideal)
```

## THE 8 STRUCTURAL PATTERNS — QUICK GUIDE

Pick the anchoring pattern before you write the first word.

**P1 — Cold Open with Tension**
Start with the reader's fear or a market fact they already know.
> *"Your board has already had the conversation."*

**P2 — Named Mechanism**
Invoke the established name. Do not describe it — name it.
> *"The Golden Sample Trap."*

**P3 — Hard Negative Definition**
Define what KritiKaal is NOT before what it IS.
> *"We are not a sourcing agency, a factory, or a marketplace."*

**P4 — Declarative Close**
4–8 words. Terminal. No elaboration.
> *"It will not ship."*

**P5 — Numeric Specificity**
Every claim → a number. No number → no claim.
> *"0% duty. £2,000 saved per £20,000 order."*

**P6 — Problem-Stack**
3–4 named pains. Then the remedy.
> *"Golden sample trap → QC gaps → timeline drift → no accountability. That is the gap we close."*

**P7 — "Here Is the Math" Pivot**
Signal the shift from narrative to numbers explicitly.
> *"Here is the math:"*

**P8 — Anti-Pitch Close**
Disqualify bad-fit prospects. This signals selectivity.
> *"If we can't create a clear advantage, we simply won't take the project."*

## TONE BY CHANNEL

| Channel | Dial | In Practice |
|---|---|---|
| Core pages | 4/10 | No filler. Every word operational. |
| Blog articles | 5/10 | One idea per paragraph. No throat-clearing. |
| LinkedIn (Yossi) | 7/10 | Opinion stated. Not hedged. Opens punchy. |
| Cold outreach | 5/10 | Pain acknowledged first. Anti-pitch close mandatory. |

---

## THE CONTRAST PIVOT (highest-converting move)

**Formula:** `"A [old model] will get you [surface benefit]. A [KritiKaal model] will get you [real outcome]."`

> *"A sourcing agent will get you a cheap price. A Managed Manufacturing partner will get you LWG compliance, SA8000 ethical standards, and sleep at night."*

Use in: pillar articles, LinkedIn posts, comparison sections, homepage copy.

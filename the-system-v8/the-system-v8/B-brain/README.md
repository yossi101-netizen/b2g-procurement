# B-brain: Your Knowledge Base

> "Brain is what you bring."

This folder holds everything YOU feed into the system. Raw inputs. Your words, your samples, your research. The stuff your agents need to know before they can do anything useful.

---

## The Boundary (Important)

**Brain vs Memory. Know the difference.**

| | B-brain | M-memory |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | Raw inputs YOU provide | Insights THE SYSTEM generates |
| **Examples** | Writing samples, worksheets, research docs, screenshots | Patterns discovered, audience reactions, strategic decisions |
| **Who fills it** | You | Your agents (after your review) |
| **Changes when** | You add new material | After every project (The Loop) |

**One sentence:** Brain is what you bring. Memory is what you learn together.

---

## What's Here

```
B-brain/
├── 01-about-me.md               → Answer questions about yourself and your work
├── 02-my-samples/           → Your past writing, organized by track
│   ├── 01-content/               → Posts, articles, newsletters (Content track)
│   ├── 02-sales/                 → Client emails, proposals (Sales track)
│   └── 03-thinking/              → Summaries, decisions, PRDs (Thinking track)
├── 03-api-keys-registry.md       → API keys for external tools (keep private)
├── 04-INBOX/                     → Drop anything new here first
└── README.md
```

---

## INBOX: Your Capture Point

**New stuff goes here first.** Screenshots, links, rough notes, voice memos, competitor examples, articles you liked. Don't overthink where it belongs. Just drop it in 04-INBOX.

Sort it later. Or ask Claude to sort it for you:

> "Read everything in B-brain/04-INBOX/ and move each item to the right subfolder. Summarize what you sorted."

The INBOX keeps your Brain clean and your capture fast.

---

## Your Samples (Pick Your Track)

Add 3 samples of your past work to the folder that matches your track:

- **Content** → `02-my-samples/01-content/` (Social posts, blog posts, articles, newsletters)
- **Sales** → `02-my-samples/02-sales/` (Client emails, proposals, follow-up messages)
- **Thinking** → `02-my-samples/03-thinking/` (Meeting summaries, decision emails, PRDs, stakeholder updates)

Each folder has a README explaining exactly what to paste, plus 3 sample files ready to fill.

---

## How Agents Use This

When creating output, agents will:
1. Study your samples to match your voice
2. Reference your 01-about-me.md for context
3. Use the relevant skill from T-tools for structure

The more you feed the brain, the smarter your agents become.

---

## Getting Started

1. **Fill in about me** - `01-about-me.md`
2. **Add samples** - Put 3 pieces in `02-my-samples/` in the subfolder matching your track
3. **Drop anything else in 04-INBOX** - Sort it later

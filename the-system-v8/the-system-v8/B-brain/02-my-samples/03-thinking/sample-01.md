

**Headline:** Responsibility is Not a Task. It is a Business Model.

**The Origin: A Hard Lesson in Supply Chain Reality** In 2012, our founder, Yossi Daniel, was producing a private-label leather line in China. The initial sample was flawless—the leather, the stitching, and the finish were exactly to specification. But when the commercial shipment arrived right before the critical holiday season, the reality was different. Uneven stitching, inconsistent leather textures, and ignored specifications ruined the batch.

When the factory was confronted, they blamed the production line. The sourcing agent claimed the issues were "within tolerance." Everyone had a plausible excuse, but as the brand owner, Yossi was the only one left dealing with refunds, chargebacks, and actual financial losses.

That failure exposed a fundamental flaw in global sourcing: The real problem isn't the factory, nor the agent. The problem is a fragmented supply chain where no single entity takes total financial and operational ownership of the final product.

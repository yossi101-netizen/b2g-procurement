## What We Are Hunting

We are not hunting for customers. We are hunting for brands experiencing the **Accountability Pain Gap**: a brand that manufactures leather goods overseas (primarily China), has no verified QC layer, and is operationally exposed to the exact failure mode KritiKaal was built to prevent.

**Hard filters — a target must pass ALL of these:**
- UK-registered company (Companies House active)
- Annual turnover: £1M–£10M (we will push higher per Founder's directive)
- Product: leather goods (bags, accessories, belts, wallets, footwear components)
- Manufacturing: overseas (China, Vietnam, Bangladesh) OR opaque (no disclosed source)
- NOT: already manufacturing in India, already LWG-certified as a buyer, craft-only or D2C marketplace-only micro brand (under £500K revenue)

**Pain signal — a target must show at least ONE of these:**
- Confirmed China import records (ImportYeti / UK customs data)
- No LWG, REACH, or ethical sourcing claim on their website
- Customer complaints about quality (Trustpilot / Google / Amazon)
- Active job posting for Sourcing Manager or QC role
- Low Fashion Transparency Index score
- Competitor has already moved to India

## The 10 Hunting Vectors (LOCKED)

### Vector 1: ImportYeti / Import Records Intelligence
**What:** US Bills of Lading (public). Cross-match UK brands that export to the US via China.
**Queries:**
- `ImportYeti: "leather goods" + shipper:China + consignee:[UK brand name]`
- `ImportYeti: "leather handbag" OR "leather wallet" OR "leather belt" + shipper:China`
- Identify UK consignee companies with confirmed China supply chain
**Output target:** 50+ UK brand names with confirmed China sourcing

### Vector 2: LinkedIn Intelligence (Decision-Maker Mapping)
**What:** Map sourcing and product decision-makers at UK leather brands. Scan their content for pain signals.
**Queries:**
- `"leather goods" + "sourcing" + United Kingdom — company size 10–200`
- `"leather" + "supply chain" + "looking for" + (India OR alternative OR partner) — UK`
- LinkedIn Jobs: `"sourcing manager" OR "QC manager" leather goods UK — posted last 90 days`
- Monitor: recent posts from identified contacts mentioning supplier, quality, China, manufacturing
**Output target:** 20–40 named decision-makers with authority profiles

### Vector 3: Trade Show Exhibitor + Buyer Lists (Lineapelle, APLF, Mipel)
**What:** Self-selected lists of brands actively investing in leather sourcing.
**Sources:**
- Lineapelle Milan (September 2026): `lineapelle-fair.it` — UK buyer registrations
- APLF Hong Kong: `aplf.com` — UK buyer list
- Mipel Milan (leather goods fair): `mipel.com` — UK brand exhibitors
- London Leather Fair (if active): UK-only domestic trade event
**Queries:**
- `site:lineapelle-fair.it buyer "United Kingdom"`
- `Lineapelle 2025 2026 UK buyer leather goods brand`
- `APLF buyer United Kingdom leather accessories 2025 2026`
**Output target:** 30–60 UK brand names with confirmed active sourcing behavior

### Vector 4: EUDR Pre-Compliance Signal Search
**What:** UK leather brands publicly signalling concern about EUDR, deforestation regulation, or supply chain documentation gaps. These are highest-intent targets — they have self-identified the exact problem KritiKaal solves.
**Queries:**
- LinkedIn: `"EUDR" OR "deforestation regulation" + "leather" + UK — posts from brand founders or sourcing directors`
- Google: `"EUDR" + "leather goods" + UK brand (2025–2026) — any press release, blog post, or trade article`
- Google Alerts: ongoing — `site:linkedin.com EUDR leather` (already configured in Authority Engine)
- Trade press: Leatherbiz, Drapers, Fashion Network UK — filter for EUDR + brand compliance coverage
- Check brand websites for EUDR or supply chain transparency statements (these signal active concern)
**Signal strength:** Any brand that has published about EUDR is a warm prospect. Any brand that has NOT published is a cold prospect with the same underlying pain.
**Output target:** 10–20 UK brands with confirmed EUDR awareness or documented compliance gap
**Note:** This vector feeds directly into Authority Engine (S6/S7/S8) — LinkedIn posts on this topic can convert awareness into inbound pipeline.**

### Vector 5: Companies House Deep Audit (P&L Intelligence)
**What:** Pull full statutory accounts — not just SIC code. Use COGS ratio to identify margin-squeezed brands.
**Execution:**
- SIC codes: 15120 (luggage/handbags manufacture), 47710 (retail clothing), 46420 (leather wholesale)
- Turnover filter: £1M–£10M (extend to £15M per Founder's directive to aim higher)
- Pull full accounts: calculate COGS ratio. Below 35% gross margin = margin pressure = supply chain pain
- Flag: any brand with declining gross margin over 2 consecutive years
**Query:**
- `beta.companieshouse.gov.uk — SIC 15120 — active — accounts filed — filter by turnover`
**Output target:** 20–40 UK companies with confirmed revenue AND margin intelligence

### Vector 6: Review Mining (Trustpilot / Google / Amazon)
**What:** Public quality complaints are a live map of a brand's accountability pain gap.
**Execution:**
- Trustpilot: search `[brand name] leather` — filter 1–3 star reviews — flag QC language
- Amazon UK: search `leather bag` or `leather wallet` — 1–2 star reviews — identify brand
- Google: `"[brand name]" leather review quality` — flag any page 1 result with defect language
**Signal phrases to flag:**
- "looks nothing like the sample" / "peeled after 3 months" / "stitching came apart"
- "wrong color" / "misleading description" / "poor quality for the price"
**Output target:** 10–20 UK brands with confirmed, public quality pain

### Vector 7: Competitor Supplier Tracking (Reverse-Engineer the Market)
**What:** If a UK brand's competitor has moved to Indian manufacturing, that brand is now competitively exposed.
**Queries:**
- `[UK leather brand competitor] India manufacturing 2024 2025 2026`
- `"made in India" leather goods UK brand 2025`
- LinkedIn: sourcing managers at UK leather brands mentioning India
- Press releases: "ethical sourcing" or "supply chain transformation" from UK leather brands
**Output target:** 5–10 brands whose competitors have moved, creating urgency

### Vector 8: Sedex / EcoVadis Member Intelligence (ELITE ADDITION)
**What:** Brands that are Sedex members or EcoVadis-rated are actively mandated to audit their supply chains. A Sedex-registered UK leather brand still sourcing from China is a near-certain high-intent prospect.
**Execution:**
- `sedexglobal.com` — search member directory for UK leather goods buyers
- EcoVadis: identify UK leather brands with published ratings — low "Environment" score = supply chain not clean
- Cross-reference Sedex membership against confirmed China sourcing (Vector 1) = highest priority targets
**Output target:** 10–20 UK brands with confirmed ethical trade mandate AND accountability gap

### Vector 9: Fashion Transparency Index + B Corp UK Registry (ELITE ADDITION)
**What:** Two databases that identify brands at opposite ends of the transparency spectrum — both are KritiKaal prospects for different reasons.
**Execution:**
- Fashion Transparency Index: `fashionrevolution.org` — UK brands scoring under 30% transparency = confirmed accountability gap
- B Corp UK Registry: `bcorporation.net/en-us/find-a-b-corp` — search "leather" + UK — these brands need ethical manufacturing partners
- Cross-reference: a brand on FTI with low score pursuing B Corp = highest urgency prospect
**Query:**
- `Fashion Transparency Index 2024 2025 UK leather brand score`
- `B Corp certified leather goods UK brand`
**Output target:** 10–15 high-intent UK brands from both ends of the transparency spectrum

### Vector 10: UKFT Member Directory + Companies House Trademark Intelligence (ELITE ADDITION)
**What:** UKFT (UK Fashion and Textile Association) is the primary trade body for UK fashion. Their member directory is a pre-qualified list of serious UK businesses. UK IPO trademark filings reveal brands that are expanding into new product categories.
**Execution:**
- UKFT: `ukft.org` — member directory — filter for leather goods companies
- UK IPO: `trademarks.ipo.gov.uk` — search class 18 (leather goods) filings from 2022–2026 — new filings = brand expanding = needs manufacturing partner
- Cross-reference UKFT members against Companies House for revenue confirmation
**Output target:** 15–20 established UK leather brands from trade body membership

## Targeting Calibration: Aim High

Per Founder's directive: do not self-limit on brand size. The COO confirms this is correct.

The KritiKaal value proposition — financial accountability, AQL 2.5, embedded QC, LWG compliance — becomes MORE compelling, not less, as brand size increases. A £5M leather goods brand has more to lose from one bad production run than a £500K brand. Their procurement teams understand accountability models. Their CFOs can read a COGS savings calculation.

**Anchor-tier targets (£150K–£500K/year deal volume) are not a stretch. They are the goal.**

The outreach strategy for Anchor-tier brands is different from Core-tier:
- Entry via trade show or warm intro (not cold email)
- First meeting: present COGS savings estimate as the opener
- Second meeting: Golden Sample test order — no financial commitment
- Third meeting: first commercial order with Escrow protection

Larger brands take longer to convert. They convert at higher value. They reorder more frequently. They refer other large brands. One Anchor-tier client ($150K+/year at 35% margin = $52K+ gross profit) is equivalent to 34 one-off $5K transactions. The math justifies the investment.

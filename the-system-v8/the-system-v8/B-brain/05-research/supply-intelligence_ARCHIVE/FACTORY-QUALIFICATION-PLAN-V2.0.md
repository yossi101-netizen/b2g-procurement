## QUESTION 3 — IMPORTYETI: OBJECTIVE COMPARISON

### 3.1 What ImportYeti Is

ImportYeti is a search interface over US Customs Bureau of Lading data — public records of goods imported into the United States by sea and air freight. It shows:
- Which Indian (and other origin) factories shipped goods to US-based importers
- Which US brands or buyers those factories serve
- Shipment frequency, volume (container counts), and dates
- Trends over time (growing, declining, or stable client relationships)

### 3.2 What You Get WITH ImportYeti ($130/month)

| Capability | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shipment verification** | Confirm whether a factory actually shipped commercial orders to US buyers — not just claimed to |
| **Client reference validation** | Cross-check factory-provided client references against actual shipping records |
| **Candidate discovery** | Find additional factories not already in the bench that ship leather goods to US buyers |
| **Volume signals** | Estimate factory capacity and utilisation from shipment frequency and container counts |
| **Competitive intelligence** | Identify which Indian factories supply KritiKaal's direct competitors |
| **Brand-factory mapping** | Discover which US brands source from which Indian clusters |

### 3.3 What You Get WITHOUT ImportYeti

| Capability | Alternative Method | Fidelity vs. ImportYeti |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Shipment verification** | Request 2 client references in the Standard Brief (already included) — verify directly with those brands via LinkedIn/email | Lower — depends on factory honesty; references can be selectively chosen |
| **Client reference validation** | LinkedIn verification of named brand relationships + direct outreach to stated buyers | Lower — cannot cross-check against actual shipping volumes |
| **Candidate discovery** | LWG database, BSCI database, CLC public directory, LinkedIn, trade show directories | Sufficient for current bench — would be insufficient if bench needs major expansion |
| **Volume signals** | Ask for production capacity in the Standard Brief — evaluate against facility size (sq ft, worker count) | Lower — self-reported figures, not independently verified |
| **Competitive intelligence** | Not available via free tools | Not available |

### 3.4 Critical Limitation of ImportYeti for KritiKaal Specifically

**ImportYeti covers US Customs data only. It does not cover UK or EU import records.**

This is the material limitation for KritiKaal's use case:
- KritiKaal's primary market is the United Kingdom and European Union
- A factory that exclusively serves UK/EU brands will not appear in ImportYeti if they do not also ship to the US
- The three Track B Kolkata factories (Dugros, Om Leather, XL Enterprises) have confirmed EU/UK export histories — but their shipment records to UK buyers are invisible in ImportYeti
- Pioneer Inc. (TA-1) ships to France, Italy, Spain — primarily EU, not US
- Apex Interglobal (TA-4) ships to UK and Germany — these records are not in ImportYeti

**Result:** ImportYeti would show a false-negative picture of KritiKaal's existing bench. Factories that are demonstrably EU/UK-export oriented would appear as unverified because their trade lane is not captured by US Customs data.

### 3.5 Objective Verdict

| Dimension | WITH ImportYeti | WITHOUT ImportYeti |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Is it mandatory?** | No | — |
| **Does it replace any gate in the Absolute Criteria Rule?** | No — certifications (G1), category (G2), export orientation (G3), maturity (G4) are all verified by free methods | — |
| **Does it add value for UK-focused sourcing?** | Partial — limited by US-only data coverage | — |
| **Does it accelerate the current process?** | No — the bench is already built and the brief is approved. ImportYeti would surface new candidates, but capacity to evaluate them is not the current bottleneck. | — |
| **When would ImportYeti become valuable?** | If the 21-Day Test fails to produce 3 qualified factories and the bench needs expansion, ImportYeti would help identify additional candidates quickly. | — |
| **Recommended timing** | After the 21-Day Test results are in. If 3+ factories qualify, ImportYeti is unnecessary. If fewer than 3 qualify, activate ImportYeti at that point. | — |

**Bottom line:** Do not subscribe to ImportYeti now. The subscription trigger is failure of the 21-Day Test to produce 3 qualifying factories. The free verification stack (LWG, BSCI, GST, MCA, LinkedIn, direct reference calls) is sufficient for the current bench.

### PHASE 4 — SAMPLE RECEIPT & AQL 2.5 EVALUATION (Week 6–8)
*Timeline: sample lead times range from 7 days (Track A, in-India) to 21 days (Track B, international shipping)*

**Step 4.1 — Sample receipt log**
- Record: factory name, tracking number, date dispatched, date received
- Photograph each sample immediately on receipt (before opening packaging)
- Note packaging quality: dust bag, packing, labelling

**Step 4.2 — AQL 2.5 evaluation — 8 criteria**
Evaluate each sample against all 8 criteria. Score each criterion: Pass / Fail / Marginal.

| Criterion | Measurement Method | Pass Condition |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1. Leather thickness | Handheld leather gauge or digital caliper | 1.2–1.4mm measured at 3 points |
| 2. Stitching density | Count stitches per inch across 3 seam sections | 8–10 SPI |
| 3. Hardware security | Pull test — manual load on each D-ring, zip, magnetic snap | No deformation, no separation at 5kg |
| 4. Edge finishing | Visual inspection of all cut edges | Fully burnished or painted — zero raw edges |
| 5. Lining attachment | Flex test — bend bag body 180° and release | No puckering, no glue bleed, no separation |
| 6. Base stud positioning | Measure stud placement from corners and from each other | Symmetric within ±5mm, flush-mounted |
| 7. Handle attachment | Load test — hang 5kg weight from each handle for 60 seconds | No stretching, no pull-through at attachment point |
| 8. Overall silhouette | Measure width, height, depth at 3 points each | Within ±5mm of specification (38×30×12cm) |

**Step 4.3 — Sample verdict**
- **Pass:** 7 or 8 criteria pass → factory advances to factory audit (Phase 5)
- **Marginal:** 5–6 criteria pass → one revision permitted before elimination
- **Fail:** 4 or fewer criteria pass → factory is eliminated

**Step 4.4 — Comparative sample report**
Produce a one-page sample comparison matrix for all evaluated factories — pass/fail per criterion, total score, ranked output.

**Deliverable at Phase 4 close:** Sample evaluation report. 2–4 factories with AQL 2.5 pass. Ranked and ready for audit phase.

### PHASE 5 — FACTORY AUDIT (Week 8–10)

**Two audit paths depending on certification status:**

**Path A — Desk audit (factory holds active BSCI/SA8000/SMETA/LWG)**
- Collect and verify all current certification documents
- Confirm expiry dates — no certificates more than 12 months from expiry accepted
- Request the most recent audit report from the certifying body (factories can request this document)
- Record: certifying body, audit date, findings summary, non-conformities (if any), corrective action status

**Path B — Physical factory audit (factory lacks active third-party certification)**
- Commission a third-party audit through QIMA, Bureau Veritas, or SGS India
- Audit scope: SMETA 4-Pillar (labour, health & safety, environment, business ethics)
- Cost: approximately $300–600 per audit, paid by KritiKaal
- Lead time: 2–3 weeks to schedule and complete
- Pass threshold: no critical non-conformities; corrective action plan accepted for major items

**Step 5.1 — Audit decision**
If a factory passed the sample evaluation (Phase 4) but lacks certification → KritiKaal's decision: invest in audit cost or eliminate. This decision requires CEO input per factory.

**Deliverable at Phase 5 close:** Audit documentation on file for each qualifying factory. Pass/fail per factory confirmed.

## Executive Summary

Week 1 deployed across Companies House, B Corp registry, Fashion Transparency Index, brand-direct web intelligence, Trustpilot review mining, and LinkedIn job signal analysis. The sweep identified **38 UK leather goods brands** at various confidence levels.

**The most important structural finding of Week 1:**

The UK leather goods market at £1M–£10M segments very differently from our initial assumption. The space is dominated by three distinct groups:

1. **Premium artisan makers** (Ettinger, Maxwell Scott, Grafea, Billy Tannery) — UK or Italian manufacturing, not China. LOW PRIORITY for KritiKaal.
2. **Mid-market DTC brands at scale** (Radley, Aspinal, Osprey, Knomo, Fiorelli) — revenue confirmed, manufacturing location **opaque or Asia-facing**. HIGHEST PRIORITY.
3. **Ethical-mandate brands** (Taylor Yates, BEEN London, ROKA London) — B Corp certified, will need verifiable ethical supply chain if they ever outsource. MEDIUM PRIORITY — long-term.

The "China Plus One" pain point is most concentrated in **group 2**: brands that have scaled beyond UK/Italian production capacity, distribute globally, price in the mid-market (£50–£300/unit), and have no disclosed supply chain accountability layer.

## Structural Finding: SIC 15120 Is the Wrong Door

Companies House SIC 15120 (Manufacture of Leather Goods) returned 96 results — of which **the overwhelming majority are dissolved micro-entities** (sole traders who registered and failed). The viable UK leather goods brands we're targeting are registered under:
- SIC 47710 (retail clothing)
- SIC 47990 (other retail)
- SIC 74100 (specialised design activities)
- SIC 46420 (wholesale clothing/footwear)

**Implication:** Week 2 Companies House research shifts from SIC 15120 to brand-name direct lookup + alternative SIC codes. The brand-direct approach (which Week 1 used from the second day) is the correct methodology.

## Critical Intelligence From Week 1 Research

### Finding 1: "Designed in Britain" ≠ "Made in Britain"

The most important signal separator found this week: **brands that say "designed in Britain" but do not claim "made in Britain" are almost certainly manufacturing overseas.** This is a deliberate legal distinction. Aspinal says "British heritage." Osprey says "British luxury." Neither says "British manufacturing." At their revenue levels, this is not an oversight — it is a supply chain disclosure strategy. Both are near-certain targets for KritiKaal.

### Finding 2: Post-Acquisition Brands Are the Highest-Opportunity Window

Fiorelli was acquired by Centric Brands in 2023. Every acquisition triggers a procurement reset — new relationships, new standards, new supplier reviews. A post-acquisition brand's sourcing team has an active mandate to "rationalize the supply chain." This is the single highest-intent window for a new supplier introduction. **Fiorelli should be Week 3's first LinkedIn mapping target.**

### Finding 3: Vida Vida Is Our Social Proof Source

Vida Vida is a UK leather goods brand that already manufactures in India (Jaipur). Their founder describes the decision in public content. This brand is not a prospect — but their story is **KritiKaal's most powerful social proof asset**. If Vida Vida (UK leather brand) chose India and has not looked back, that de-risks the India decision for every other UK leather brand we approach. The Vida Vida story should be incorporated into every outreach narrative.

### Finding 4: The Wolf & Badger Platform Is a Prospect Goldmine

Wolf & Badger is a £100M+ curated marketplace for independent fashion brands, with stores in London, NYC, and LA. Their leather accessories brand roster is exactly our ICP — small-to-mid UK brands, premium positioning, global reach, no disclosed manufacturing partner. **Week 2 priority: scrape the Wolf & Badger leather brand directory and cross-reference every brand against Companies House for revenue data.**

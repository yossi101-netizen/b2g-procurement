## 0. What this tool is, and what it is not (read first)
Wispr Flow is a voice dictation tool. It converts speech into polished text.
It does NOT find leads, send messages, or close deals. It automates nothing.
- Source check: in the "Money Machine" video, the actual sales automation was
  Claude + Emergent + hex.tech. Wispr Flow's only role there was DICTATING the
  prompts to Claude. The CEO's words: "I would never have typed a prompt as
  detailed as this out."
- The "1,000 deals a month" claim is low-ticket, product-led software signups
  with zero-friction self-serve onboarding. It does NOT transfer to KritiKaal:
  physical goods, MOQ 300+, Golden Sample, compliance, high-trust, ~28 clients
  per YEAR. Do not chase that number. By the video's own rule, high-ticket sales
  require a human in the loop.

## 1. The single highest-value use-case (the reframe)
DICTATION FRONT-END FOR PROMPTING. Yossi thinks in long, specific, numbers-heavy
instructions. Speaking a 400-word brief to an AI or into an email takes ~90
seconds; typing it takes 8 minutes. This is where Wispr Flow earns its place.

## 2. Realistic KritiKaal use-cases (ranked)
1. Dictating detailed prompts and briefs (to Claude, to research, to drafts).
2. Rapid warm follow-ups. Reply to an engaged prospect in 30 seconds of speech.
   Latency loses trust-based deals.
3. Hyper-personalized first-touch outreach, one message per prospect after
   reading their site or LinkedIn. Never a blast.
4. Long-form replies: tech-pack answers, compliance explanations (REACH Cr(VI),
   Prop 65, EUDR), shipping and duty breakdowns, dictated in full sentences.
5. CRM and Daily-Log notes dictated straight after a call while fresh.

NOT a use-case: mass identical sends, autogenerated cold copy, anything that
removes the founder's judgment from the message.

## 3. Configure Wispr Flow to obey Voice DNA
Step 1: Install on the primary device. Use the Enterprise tier if client-
        confidential audio will pass through it (SOC 2 Type II). Decide the data
        boundary BEFORE dictating client names, pricing, or compliance details.
Step 2: Auto-edit. Set to the lightest setting or OFF. Reason: its cleanup pass
        inserts em-dashes and generic smoothing. Em-dashes are banned, absolutely.
Step 3: Personal Dictionary. Add: KritiKaal, Golden Sample, AQL 2.5, Double-Back
        Guarantee, Single Point of Accountability, The Missing Middle, LWG Gold,
        EUDR, REACH, Cr(VI), DCTS, Kanpur, Kolkata, Agra, Nhava Sheva, plus every
        active prospect and company name. Stops mis-transcription of the exact
        named mechanisms doctrine forbids paraphrasing.
Step 4: Snippets. Voice shortcuts for repeated assets ONLY (calendar link,
        sample-kit one-pager, compliance one-pager). Never snippet a full message.
Step 5: Spoken-voice discipline. Short sentences. State numbers. Open with a
        concrete moment or a specific problem. Say who this is NOT for. No
        "leading," "premier," "world-class" without a metric.
Step 6: Hebrew test. Before using on the Israel channel, dictate 10 mixed
        Hebrew/English WhatsApp messages and check accuracy and RTL. If quality
        is poor, restrict Wispr Flow to US/UK English channels for now.
Step 7: G6 Gatekeeper stays the final filter on anything public-facing. Manual
        em-dash scan on every message until config trust is high.

## 4. Fit into the demand-gen pipeline (leads.db / Israel-Hunter V3)
Wispr Flow sits at the COMPOSE / PROMPT step only. The pipeline is unchanged:
  census -> bol_pipeline -> digest -> leads.db (Class A/B/C) -> [RESEARCH lead]
  -> [DICTATE prompt/message with Wispr Flow] -> [SEND in WhatsApp/Email/LinkedIn]
  -> [LOG outcome to CRM / Daily-Log]
- leads.db stays CORE-only. Wispr Flow does not touch, read, or write to it.
- Wispr Flow is not a sequencer or CRM. If those layers do not exist, a dictation
  tool does not fill that gap. Name the missing layer separately.

## 5. Daily founder workflow (30 to 45 min outreach block)
Morning, 20 min:
  - Open Daily-Log Decision Dashboard. Pull priority leads from leads.db.
  - For each: read 2 min of context (site, LinkedIn, recent trigger event).
  - Dictate one personalized first-touch. Manual em-dash scan. Send.
Midday, 10 min:
  - Clear warm replies first. Dictate follow-ups in real sentences. Speed wins.
End of day, 5 to 10 min:
  - Dictate CRM and Daily-Log notes while fresh. Feed one real-world update in.

## 6. KPIs (track these, not vanity volume)
- Personalized touches per day (sustainable target, e.g. 30 to 60).
- Reply rate per market.
- Meetings or sample-kit requests booked.
- Compose time per message (should fall 3x to 5x).
Do NOT track "closes per month" against the 1,000 fantasy. Track 2 to 3 closes
per month against the $1M ARR plan (28 clients x $35K).

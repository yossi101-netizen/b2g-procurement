# COMPLIANCE & LABELING SOP
## Bangalore Facility — India → US Export
### Zero-Tolerance Standard. Every unit. Every shipment.

---

> **Ground rule:** CBP does not issue warnings. A non-compliant shipment is held,
> seized, or destroyed. An FTC violation is a civil penalty of **up to $50,120 per
> violation per day**. Amazon's response to an IP or safety flag is instant
> delisting and capital freeze. This SOP is not aspirational — it is the
> minimum threshold for legal US commerce.

---

## PART A — CATEGORY-SPECIFIC LABELING REQUIREMENTS

---

### CATEGORY 1: HOME TEXTILES
*(Cushion covers, throws, bed sheets, table linen, napkins, curtains)*

**Governing law:**
- FTC Care Labeling Rule — 16 CFR Part 423
- Textile Fiber Products Identification Act (TFPIA) — 15 U.S.C. §§68–68j; 16 CFR Part 303
- Country of Origin — 19 U.S.C. §1304; 19 CFR Part 134

---

#### LABEL A — COMBINED FIBER CONTENT + CARE + COO LABEL
**Format:** Permanently sewn-in woven or printed label. NOT a hang tag.
**Placement:** Inside the article, accessible without destructive removal.
  - Pillow/cushion cover: inside seam, center back
  - Bed sheet: center hem at foot end
  - Table linen: center back hem
  - Throw/blanket: center back hem or lower corner

**Required text (exact wording):**

```
[LINE 1]  100% COTTON              ← Fiber content, generic name, % by weight
[LINE 2]  IMPORTED BY [COMPANY]    ← Your legal company name OR your FTC RN Number
[LINE 3]  MADE IN INDIA            ← Country of origin, exactly this phrasing
[LINE 4]  ─────────────────────────
[LINE 5]  CARE INSTRUCTIONS:
[LINE 6]  MACHINE WASH WARM
[LINE 7]  TUMBLE DRY LOW
[LINE 8]  WARM IRON IF NEEDED
[LINE 9]  DO NOT BLEACH
[LINE 10] DO NOT DRY CLEAN
```

**Mandatory field rules:**
| Field | Rule | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Fiber content | Generic name + % weight. "100% Cotton" ✓ / "Cotton Blend" ✗ | FTC citation |
| Company ID | Full legal company name OR FTC Registered Number (RN#). Apply for RN at ftc.gov | FTC citation |
| Country of origin | "Made in India" — no abbreviations, no "Product of India" | CBP hold + fine |
| Care symbols | May substitute ASTM D5489 symbols for written text — but must include ALL 5 care categories | FTC citation |
| Language | English mandatory. Spanish optional | — |

**Label specifications:**
- Minimum font size: 6-point (about 2mm cap height)
- Must be legible after 50 home launderings
- Must be colorfast (no dye bleed onto garment)
- Preferred: white woven label, black ink

---

#### LABEL B — AMAZON FNSKU BARCODE (FBA channel only)
**Placement:** Exterior of polybag, upper-right corner. Must be scannable with polybag sealed.
- Do NOT place on the product itself unless it can be easily peeled without damage
- Do NOT cover any product labeling
- Size: minimum 1" × 2"

---

#### LABEL C — SUFFOCATION WARNING (FBA channel — polybag)
Required by Amazon if polybag opening ≥ 5 inches when laid flat.

**Exact text (16pt minimum font OR warning symbol):**
```
WARNING: To avoid danger of suffocation, keep this plastic bag away from
babies and children. Do not use in cribs, beds, carriages or playpens.
```

**Placement:** Front face of polybag, prominently visible.

---

#### TEXTILE COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — Unit Level
```
□ Sewn-in care + fiber content + COO label present and legible
□ Label is permanent (cannot be removed without cutting)
□ Fiber content uses ONLY generic names (Cotton, Polyester, etc.)
□ Percentage of each fiber stated
□ Company name OR RN number present
□ "Made in India" — exact phrasing, not abbreviated
□ All 5 care categories addressed (wash / dry / iron / bleach / dry-clean)
□ FNSKU barcode on exterior (FBA only)
□ Polybag suffocation warning present (FBA only, if applicable)
□ No retail pricing printed anywhere on product or packaging
```

### CATEGORY 2: WOOD & BRASS DÉCOR
*(Figurines, picture frames, candle holders, decorative objects, trays)*

**Governing law:**
- Country of Origin — 19 U.S.C. §1304; 19 CFR Part 134
- California Prop 65 — Cal. Health & Safety Code §25249.5; 27 CCR §25600 et seq.
- CPSC General Certificate of Conformity (if any item could reach a child)
- CARB ATCM 93120 (formaldehyde in composite wood — MDF, plywood, particleboard)

---

#### PROP 65 — THE NON-NEGOTIABLE FOR BRASS AND ALL METAL DÉCOR

**Why brass always triggers this:** Standard brass alloys (CuZn) contain up to 3% lead
as a machinability additive. The Prop 65 safe-harbor threshold for lead is
**0.5 micrograms/day oral exposure**. Decorative brass pieces handled repeatedly
(figurines, trays, bowls) routinely exceed this threshold. **Assume all brass requires
a Prop 65 warning unless you have a test report proving otherwise.**

**Testing protocol (mandatory before first shipment of any brass/metal SKU):**
1. Send 3 representative samples to an ILAC-accredited US lab (SGS, Intertek, Bureau Veritas, or equivalent)
2. Test protocol: California Prop 65 Metals Panel — lead, cadmium, phthalates
3. Test turnaround: 5–10 business days
4. Accept/reject: if any metal exceeds Prop 65 threshold → warning label required

**The Prop 65 warning (mandatory 2018-format):**
```
⚠ WARNING: This product can expose you to lead, which is known
to the State of California to cause birth defects or other
reproductive harm. For more information go to
www.P65Warnings.ca.gov
```

**Placement:**
- Affixed to the product via permanent hang tag, sticker, or packaging
- Must be "clearly and conspicuously" displayed
- Minimum: 6-point font
- For Amazon listings: this exact text (or the symbol + short form) must appear in the product listing bullet points AND in the legal compliance section of the listing

**IMPORTANT:** Prop 65 warnings are NOT admission of guilt. They are compliance.
Selling without a warning when you need one is the violation. Selling with one when
you don't need one is not.

---

#### LABEL A — COUNTRY OF ORIGIN (ALL WOOD/BRASS DÉCOR)
**For wood items:** "Made in India" — printed, stamped, engraved, or painted directly
on the article in a conspicuous location. Hang tag acceptable ONLY if the article is
too small to permanently mark or if permanent marking would damage the article.

**For brass items:** Same — engraved or stamped "Made in India" on the base or
underside. Must be indelible.

**If article is too small to mark:** The immediate container (gift box, inner sleeve)
must bear "Made in India."

---

#### FORMALDEHYDE — COMPOSITE WOOD (MDF/plywood/particleboard frames)
**CARB ATCM 93120** limits formaldehyde emissions for composite wood sold in California.
This applies to picture frames with MDF/plywood components.

**Required action:** Confirm with supplier that materials are CARB Phase 2 compliant.
Get a written declaration from the supplier. This is a declaration, not a test —
reputable mills supply it routinely. File it. Never ship MDF/plywood frames without it.

---

#### WOOD/BRASS COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — Unit Level
```
□ "Made in India" permanently marked on article (or immediate container if too small)
□ Prop 65 warning label present on ALL brass/metal items (unless test report clears it)
□ Test report on file for Prop 65 (lead, cadmium panel) from ILAC-accredited lab
□ If MDF/plywood: CARB Phase 2 supplier declaration on file
□ No child-appeal features without CPSC testing (avoid this entirely)
□ Prop 65 language in Amazon listing bullets (FBA channel)
□ FNSKU on polybag exterior (FBA only)
```

### CATEGORY 3: LEATHER GOODS
*(Wallets, passport covers, bags, belts, small accessories)*

**Governing law:**
- Country of Origin — 19 U.S.C. §1304
- California Prop 65 — cadmium (dyes), lead (metal hardware), hexavalent chromium (tanning)
- No federal fiber content law applies to leather

---

#### PROP 65 — LEATHER-SPECIFIC RISKS

Three chemical pathways in leather goods:

| Source | Chemical | Prop 65 Listed | Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chrome tanning (wet-blue leather) | Hexavalent Chromium (Cr VI) | Yes — carcinogen | 0.05 μg/day |
| Metal hardware (zippers, snaps, buckles, eyelets) | Lead | Yes | 0.5 μg/day |
| Colorants/dyes | Cadmium | Yes | 0.03 μg/day |

**Testing protocol:**
- Leather panel: Cr VI by ISO 17075
- Metal hardware: Lead by XRF screening (fast, inexpensive) — full ICP if XRF positive
- Dyed leather: Cadmium by ICP
- Minimum: one test per leather-type + colorway per supplier. Retest if supplier changes tannery or dye batch.

**Practical reality on Cr VI:** Well-produced Indian leather (Kanpur, Chennai) from reputable tanneries is usually REACH-compliant on Cr VI. Ask the tanner directly for their ISO 17075 test certificate — established tanneries have these. If they can't produce one, find a different tannery.

---

#### LABEL A — COUNTRY OF ORIGIN
- Stamped, embossed, or printed inside the article: "Made in India"
- On wallets: interior pocket lining
- On bags: interior label tag
- Must be indelible — not a sticker that peels

---

#### LABEL B — MATERIAL DESCRIPTION (Amazon / FBA requirement)
Amazon requires accurate material descriptions in the listing. For leather:
- "Genuine Leather" = full-grain, top-grain, or corrected-grain leather ✓
- "Bonded leather" = must be disclosed ✗ (do not sell bonded leather as "genuine")
- If hardware is present: list hardware material (zinc alloy, brass, etc.)

**FTC guidelines on leather:** "Genuine leather" may only describe products made
from actual animal hides. Bonded/reconstituted leather (< 50% natural fiber) must
be disclosed.

---

#### LEATHER COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — Unit Level
```
□ "Made in India" permanently marked inside article
□ Prop 65 test report for Cr VI (leather) on file
□ Prop 65 test report for lead (hardware, if any) on file
□ Prop 65 warning label if any threshold exceeded
□ Material accurately described — no "genuine leather" on bonded/PU
□ No misleading brand marks or logos on article or hardware
□ FNSKU on polybag exterior (FBA only)
```

**Amazon FBA additional carton requirement (FBA channel):**
- Amazon Shipment ID label on each carton (generated in Seller Central)
- "AMAZON FBA" marked on carton
- Carton content label with FNSKU, quantity, condition

**Do NOT write on cartons:**
- Any price information
- "Made in China" (even as a template error) — immediate CBP red flag
- Third-party brand names

## PART C — TESTING & DOCUMENTATION ARCHIVE

Maintain a physical + digital file for each SKU containing:

| Document | Required for | When to renew |
|---|---|---|
| Prop 65 test report (metals panel) | ALL brass/metal décor, leather goods | New supplier, new colorway, every 12 months |
| CARB Phase 2 declaration | MDF/plywood composite wood | New supplier |
| ISO 17075 Cr VI test (leather) | All chrome-tanned leather | New tannery, new batch |
| Third-party QC inspection report | Every shipment ≥$500 value | Per shipment |
| Supplier invoice (original) | Amazon IP complaints, CBP entry | Keep 5 years |
| Packing list | All shipments | Per shipment |
| Commercial invoice | CBP formal entry | Per shipment |
| Bill of lading / AWB | All shipments | Per shipment |
| Label photos (per SKU, per lot) | CBP + Amazon audit trail | Per lot |

**Testing lab list (ILAC-accredited, India offices):**
- SGS India Pvt Ltd — sgs.com/india
- Intertek India — intertek.com/india
- Bureau Veritas India — bureauveritas.co.in
- TÜV SÜD South Asia — tuvsud.com/en-in

Turnaround: 5–10 business days standard / 2–3 days express.
Budget: $80–$150 per test panel per SKU (lead/cadmium/Cr VI).

## PART D — AMAZON LISTING COMPLIANCE (FBA channel)

For every SKU routed to Amazon FBA, the product listing must contain:

**In bullet points:**
- Exact fiber content (textiles): "100% Cotton"
- Country of origin: "Made in India"
- Prop 65 warning (if applicable): full 2018-format text
- Care instructions (textiles): at minimum, washing and drying

**In product details:**
- Material: accurate description
- Country of Origin field: India
- Brand: YOUR brand name (not the supplier's, not the factory's)

**Do NOT put in listing:**
- Competitor brand names in keywords or description (trademark violation)
- Claims you cannot substantiate ("hypoallergenic," "organic," "lead-free" without test cert)
- "FDA approved" — textiles/décor are not FDA-regulated; the phrase creates false impressions

## PART E — NON-CONFORMANCE PROCEDURE

If any unit fails inspection at the facility:

1. **Label defect** (missing, wrong text, not permanent):
   → QUARANTINE the lot
   → Remediate: apply corrective labels and re-inspect 100% of units
   → Do NOT ship until 100% of lot passes
   → Document corrective action in lot file

2. **Prop 65 test fail** (above threshold):
   → Two options: (a) apply warning label to all units in lot, or (b) renegotiate return to supplier
   → Option (a) is faster and cheaper; the warning is not a sales killer on Amazon if properly formatted
   → Update Amazon listing with warning language before first unit ships

3. **COO missing from article** (not just carton):
   → CBP can fine $1,000–$10,000 per shipment + require rework or destruction
   → Remediate at facility before shipment — cheaper than CBP detention
   → Stenciled or stamped correction acceptable with photographic evidence

4. **Test report not on file at time of shipment:**
   → Do not ship. Period.
   → A CBP exam that finds no test documentation on Prop 65 goods results in detainment pending laboratory analysis at importer's cost.
